PLAN YOUR TRIP TO ENJOY THE BEST

1.

Losar

Time of Year: February/March

Losar, the Tibetan New Year, is one of the most anticipated festivals in Sikkim, celebrated with immense enthusiasm by the Buddhist communities. The festival usually spans over 15 days, with the first three days holding the greatest significance. Preparations begin weeks in advance, with homes being thoroughly cleaned and decorated to ward off evil spirits and welcome the new year with auspiciousness.

2.

Saga Dawa

Time of Year: May/June

Saga Dawa is a profoundly sacred festival for Buddhists in Sikkim, commemorating the three most important events of Lord Buddha's life: his birth, enlightenment (nirvana), and attainment of parinirvana (passing away). This month-long festival is a time for deep reflection, meritorious deeds, and religious observance.

3.

Pang Lhabsol

Time of Year: August/September

Unique to Sikkim, Pang Lhabsol is a festival that pays homage to the state's guardian deity, Mount Khangchendzonga. It also commemorates the blood brotherhood treaty sworn between the Lepchas and the Bhutias in the 13th century. This festival is a powerful symbol of unity and the deep reverence the Sikkimese people have for their natural surroundings.

4.

Losoong

Time of Year: December

Losoong marks the end of the harvest season and the beginning of the Sikkimese New Year. It is a time of joyous celebration for the Bhutia and Lepcha communities. The festival provides a well-deserved respite for farmers after a year of hard work, and the celebrations are a vibrant expression of their gratitude for a bountiful harvest.
